About Me

Iulian Furtuna is a multidisciplinary artist whose career spans acting, mime, puppetry, theater direction, and filmmaking. From his early days with Footsbarn Theater to directing award-winning short films like Les Colocataires for Canal+, Iulian has consistently pushed the boundaries of storytelling. His recent co-directed documentary, Le Peuple des Nuages, explores the intersection of culture and nature. A pioneer in 3D and holographic art, Iulian also shares his expertise as a teacher, having taught animation, drama, and film at institutions like EICAR Paris, RSAMD Glasgow, and ArtCollege Edinburgh.

Filmography

Les Colocataires

A humorous and poignant short film exploring the dynamics of shared living spaces, produced for Canal+.

Le Peuple des Nuages

A co-directed documentary that delves into the lives and traditions of a remote community, blending stunning visuals with intimate storytelling.

Theater & Performances

From my early days with Footsbarn Theater to international stages, I have explored the expressive power of mime and puppetry. These art forms allow me to communicate universal emotions without words, creating a unique connection with audiences.

3D & Holograms

One of my most ambitious projects involved creating a holographic performance that merged traditional puppetry with cutting-edge technology. The result was a mesmerizing experience that blurred the line between reality and illusion.

Teaching

Teaching is about inspiring the next generation of artists to explore their creativity and push boundaries. My approach combines technical skill-building with a focus on storytelling and innovation.
